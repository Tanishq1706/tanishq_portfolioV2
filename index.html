KTM 390 Duke · Youth Digital Campaign · 2025
Target Audience
18–26 year-old male riders in Tier 1 & 2 cities. Engineering students, first-job earners. Influenced by motorsport, streetwear, and international bike culture. Currently riding 150–200cc but aspirationally stretching to 390cc.
Core Message
The 390 Duke isn't a step up. It's a transformation. You don't upgrade to it — you become it.
Campaign Insight
KTM's current India content plays it safe with spec sheets and track footage. The real purchase trigger for this segment isn't performance data — it's identity. They want to feel like a racer before they buy. Content should make them see themselves in the bike.
4-Week Content Calendar
Week 1
Teaser Reels — 5 × 15-sec anonymous rider clips with no branding. "Something is coming." Identity intrigue.
Week 2
Identity posts — 5 × Instagram carousels asking "What kind of rider are you?" leading to a Duke reveal. Engagement-first.
Week 3
YouTube hero video — 90-sec "first ride" film. Raw, urban, no VO. Music-driven. User reaction UGC push.
Week 4
Community takeover — partner with 3 micro-influencer riders (50–150K). Campus test ride events. LinkedIn SEO content targeting engineering college audience.

Bajaj Pulsar NS400 · Brand Repositioning · 2025
Strategic Problem
Bajaj Pulsar is the most familiar name in Indian motorcycles — but familiarity has become a liability. It's seen as "dad's bike" by Gen Z even as the NS400 is a genuinely premium machine. The brand needs a repositioning story, not just better ads.
Campaign Insight
Every Indian city kid grew up seeing Pulsars. That's not a weakness — it's the foundation of the boldest possible narrative: "You grew up watching it. Now it's time to catch up." Reclaim ownership. Nostalgia + aspiration fused.
Core Message
The Pulsar didn't stand still. Did you?
Content Strategy
Phase 1
Nostalgia hook — "First Pulsar Memory" UGC campaign. Ask Instagram followers to share their first Pulsar sighting. Free reach, massive emotional engagement.
Phase 2
The "catch up" reveal — side-by-side evolution video series. 2004 Pulsar vs NS400. Data specs told as a character evolution story.
Phase 3
Performance content — track day events in 5 cities, filmed for YouTube and Reels. Authentic, not staged. NS400 rider profiles — engineers, designers, creators.
Phase 4
SEO content push — "NS400 vs [competitor]" comparison articles, buyer guides, and YouTube review partnerships with mid-tier moto channels.

// 04 — Strategic Brand Analysis

Brand
Teardown

How I see the three brands I want to work with — strengths, gaps, and where I'd focus first.

KTM
Positioning: Aggressive Performance · Youth-First
What Works
International brand equity travels well. "Ready to Race" is one of the cleanest positioning lines in global motorcycling. Strong aspirational pull in Tier 1 cities. Duke 390 is genuinely world-class hardware.
What's Broken
India content is spec-heavy and emotionally flat. Social feeds are dominated by track footage that most Indian buyers will never access. The gap between the brand story and the buyer's reality is too large. Vernacular content: nearly zero.
Opportunity
Identity-led content. Build around the transformation of becoming a KTM rider, not the bike's specs. Tier 2 city community building. College campus events with content creation.
My First Move
A "First KTM" UGC series on Instagram. Real riders. Real cities. No racetrack required. Builds community and generates content at near-zero cost.
BAJAJ
Positioning: Performance Value · Mass Premium
What Works
Unmatched distribution and trust. Pulsar is the most recognised performance brand name in India. The NS400 is a genuinely strong product that punches well above its price point. CNG bike innovation is forward-thinking.
What's Broken
Brand perception hasn't kept up with product evolution. The Pulsar name carries "affordable" baggage when the NS400 deserves premium positioning. Digital content is inconsistent — strong in some markets, weak in youth segments.
Opportunity
Nostalgia-to-aspiration repositioning. The Pulsar's history is a brand asset no competitor has. A "from your childhood to your driveway" narrative could convert Gen Z buyers who grew up with the brand.
My First Move
Launch an NS400 comparison content series — honest, SEO-optimised, YouTube + blog — targeting every competitive keyword the buyer searches before purchase. Own the consideration stage.
HERO
Positioning: Trusted Mass Market · India's Commuter
What Works
Scale and distribution that no Indian brand can match. 6,000+ dealers. Deep trust in rural and semi-urban India. The brand genuinely is India's workhorse — that's a powerful narrative foundation when told right.
What's Broken
Digital content is corporate and disconnected from actual buyers. No strong SEO presence for the keywords their buyers actually use. Vernacular digital marketing is underdeveloped relative to the size of the market Hero serves.
Opportunity
Human storytelling at scale. Hero's 6,000 dealers are 6,000 content distribution nodes. A dealer co-marketing programme with simple content toolkits could generate enormous grassroots digital reach at very low cost.
My First Move
A Hindi + English SEO content hub targeting Tier 2/3 city buyer queries — "best mileage bike under ₹80,000", "Splendor vs Shine", "best bike for long distance commute". Rank for what Hero's buyers are actually searching.
